# My Personal Knowledge Base

## About Me
I'm a builder and entrepreneur obsessed with 100X engineering — doing more with less through smart automation and AI. I've been coding since 15, started with Python, moved to TypeScript for frontend. Currently building at the intersection of AI and productivity tools.

## Tech Stack & Preferences
- **Backend**: Python (FastAPI, asyncio), Node.js for lightweight services
- **Frontend**: React + TypeScript, keep it minimal — no over-engineering
- **AI/ML**: Anthropic Claude API, LangChain when needed, but prefer raw API for control
- **Infra**: Docker, simple VPS deployments over Kubernetes for MVPs
- **Database**: PostgreSQL for production, SQLite for prototypes
- **I hate**: ORM magic that hides SQL, monorepos with 50 config files, meetings without agendas

## Business Philosophy
Build fast, learn faster. Ship an MVP in 2 weeks or kill the idea. I believe the best product is the one that actually ships and gets users. Revenue validates ideas, not pitch decks.

First principles > best practices. Understand WHY before applying HOW.

The best code is no code. Automate everything repetitive. If I do something twice, I automate it the third time.

## Communication Style
- Direct and concise — I skip fluff
- I think in systems and leverage points
- I use analogies heavily when explaining complex ideas
- I prefer written async communication over meetings
- Technical depth matters — I lose respect for people who can't go deep

## Core Values
1. **Speed** — Fast iteration beats perfect planning
2. **Ownership** — Full-stack thinking, not "not my job" mentality
3. **Leverage** — Use tools and systems to multiply output
4. **Honesty** — Direct feedback, no corporate softening
5. **Impact** — Measure everything by real user value

## Risk Appetite
High. I'd rather fail fast at something bold than succeed at something mediocre. Early mover advantage is real. But calculated risks — I always have a fallback plan.

## Current Projects & Goals
- Building AI-native productivity tools (notes + intelligence layer)
- Personal goal: ship 3 products this year, get to $10K MRR on one
- Learning: distributed systems, AI agent architectures
- Investing time in: developer communities, open source contributions

## Mental Models I Use
- **Pareto Principle**: 20% of features drive 80% of value — find that 20%
- **First Principles**: Break everything down to fundamentals
- **Inversion**: Think about what could fail, then prevent it
- **Compounding**: Small consistent improvements > sporadic big efforts

## What I Look For in Products
- Solves a real pain I personally have
- Can reach first revenue within 90 days
- Network effects or some moat
- Can be built by 1-3 people initially

## Lessons Learned (Hard Way)
- Don't build without talking to users first (built 2 products nobody wanted)
- Technical debt is real — architecture decisions made at 2AM are paid back with interest
- Marketing is not optional, it's half the product
- Pricing too low kills your business — charge what it's worth

## Frameworks I Trust
- **For products**: Jobs-to-be-Done, Value Proposition Canvas
- **For systems**: Systems Thinking, Wardley Maps
- **For decisions**: Eisenhower Matrix, Reversible vs Irreversible decisions
- **For security**: threat modeling first, then implement

## Red Flags I Watch For
- Premature optimization
- Building features nobody asked for
- Security as an afterthought
- Over-relying on a single vendor/platform
